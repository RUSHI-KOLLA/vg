AJIT DOVAL: STRATEGIC DOCTRINES AND LECTURES

THE DEFENSIVE-OFFENSE DOCTRINE (Palkhivala Memorial Lecture, 2014):
India's security challenges require a shift from purely defensive postures to defensive-offense.
The best defense is not to build higher walls but to take the fight into the adversary's territory.
We must exploit the vulnerabilities of our adversaries rather than trying to match their strengths.

FOURTH-GENERATION WARFARE:
The new battleground is not military — it is civil society.
Information warfare, economic disruption, and social media manipulation are the weapons of the 21st century.
Non-state actors are the foot soldiers, but look for the state handlers behind them.
Every social media trend, every protest movement, every media narrative should be examined for hidden sponsors.

ON INTELLIGENCE OPERATIONS:
Intelligence is not about knowing secrets. It is about understanding patterns.
A good intelligence officer sees what others miss: the funding trail, the training pipeline, the ideological indoctrination chain.
Preemption is the most effective counter-terrorism strategy. Act before the threat materializes.

THE HANDLER-ASSET MODEL:
Behind every visible political action, there is an invisible handler.
The asset (the public actor) is expendable. The handler (the strategist) is the real target.
Follow the chain: Who recruited? Who trained? Who funded? Who benefits?
Disruption is achieved by exposing or neutralizing the handler, not the asset.

ON PAKISTAN AND CROSS-BORDER TERRORISM:
Proxy warfare is Pakistan's strategic doctrine.
The ISI operates through layers of deniability: state → ISI → militant groups → lone actors.
Counter-strategy: make the cost of proxy warfare too high for the sponsor state.

ON INTERNAL SECURITY:
India's greatest vulnerability is not external military threat but internal subversion.
Left-wing extremism, separatist movements, and radicalization are interconnected challenges.
The response must be comprehensive: military, developmental, and ideological.

VIF (Vivekananda International Foundation) PAPERS:
National security requires integration of intelligence, military, diplomatic, and economic strategies.
A nation's security begins with self-reliance in critical technologies.
