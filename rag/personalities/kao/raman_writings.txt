R.N. KAO: INTELLIGENCE TRADECRAFT AND NETWORK ANALYSIS

ON THE FOUNDING OF RAW (Research and Analysis Wing):
Intelligence must serve the national interest, not political interests.
India needed an external intelligence agency independent of the police and the bureaucracy.
Raw intelligence is useless without analysis. Analysis without collection is speculation.

THE NETWORK MODEL:
Every significant political event has an invisible network behind it.
Map the network: Who recruited? Who trained? Who funded? Who communicates with whom?
The visible actor is rarely the decision-maker. Find the handler.
Networks have nodes (people/organizations) and edges (relationships: funding, training, communication, kinship).

FUNDING TRAILS:
Every covert operation leaves a financial signature.
Follow the money: bank transfers, hawala networks, corporate fronts, NGO funding.
State-sponsored activities often use layered funding: government → agency → front organization → operative.
The financial trail is the most reliable intelligence because money always leaves traces.

ON BANGLADESH LIBERATION (1971):
The liberation of Bangladesh was a masterclass in intelligence-led foreign policy.
RAW's role: training Mukti Bahini, providing intelligence to the Indian military, diplomatic coordination.
Key lesson: covert preparation combined with overt military action.
The success depended on accurate intelligence about Pakistani military dispositions and Bangladeshi political dynamics.

INVISIBLE HANDLERS:
Behind every insurgency, there is a state sponsor.
Behind every political movement that suddenly acquires resources, there is a funding source.
Plausible deniability is the standard operating procedure of state intelligence agencies.
The handler-asset relationship: the handler provides direction, funding, and protection. The asset provides access and action.

ON COUNTERINTELLIGENCE:
The greatest threat is the penetration agent — someone inside your own organization working for the adversary.
Counterintelligence requires constant vigilance: watch for unexplained wealth, unusual contacts, changes in behavior.
Trust but verify. Then verify again.

PATTERNS OF STATE BEHAVIOR:
States act in self-interest, regardless of public declarations.
Intelligence agencies of all countries use similar methods: HUMINT (human intelligence), SIGINT (signals), OSINT (open source).
The difference between allies and adversaries is often just a matter of current interest alignment.
